DEALS & 
TRADES
You can buy, sell, or swap 
heroes with other players at 
any time. 
You must sell all buildings in a 
color set to the Bank before you 
can sell or trade a hero. You 
cannot sell or trade buildings to 
another player.
Heroes can be traded for cash, 
other heroes, and/or Get Out of 
Jail Free cards. The amount is 
decided by the players making 
the deal.
Sidelined heroes can be traded 
at any agreed-upon price.
The new owner must 
immediately:
Reactivate the hero 
(pay the Bank the cost shown 
on the card).
Or keep them sidelined 
(just pay the Bank 10% of that 
cost now). Money cannot be collected on sidelined 
heroes. However, the increased payment 
can be collected on the active heroes in a 
color set.
The increased payment on Winter Soldier 
and Black Panther may be collected in 
the same way.

Selling Buildings
Sell Headquarters to the Bank for half the cost 
price, and exchange them immediately for 4 Bases. 
Sell Bases to the Bank for half the cost price. 
Bases must be sold evenly across the color set.
Sidelining (Mortgaging) Heroes
To sideline a hero, you must first sell all buildings 
in their color set to the Bank at half their cost 
price.
To sideline, turn the hero card facedown, and 
collect the sideline value on the back from the 
Bank. 
To reactivate a hero, pay the Bank the cost 
shown on the card; then, turn the card faceup.

Travel around the board drafting as many heroes as you can. The more heroes you draft, the more 
money you can collect, and the more powerful you’ll be when you battle the Children of Thanos.

Building Headquarters
Once you have 4 Bases on all hero spaces in a color 
set, you can build a Headquarters.
Pay the Headquarters cost on the hero card, return all 
4 Bases to the Bank, and put a Headquarters on the 
hero’s space.
You can only have 1 Headquarters per hero. You can’t 
add any more Bases.
You cannot build on a hero’s space if any hero in their 
color set is sidelined.
Not enough buildings?
If multiple players want to buy the last Base or Headquarters, the Banker 
must auction it. Bids start at 10 and anyone can increase the bid by as 
little as 1. You don’t need to follow turn order. Payment goes to the Bank. 
No buildings left? 
You can’t buy any until someone  sells theirs back.

In this version of Monopoly, instead of buying properties, you’ll draft heroes for your team. 
Heroes come in color sets, except for Winter Soldier and Black Panther.
Undrafted Heroes
When you land on a hero that has not been drafted, you must draft them or auction their allegiance.
Drafted Heroes
When you land on a hero that someone else has drafted, the owner must ask you for payment. If they do, 
you must pay. If they don’t ask before the next player rolls the dice, you don’t have to pay!

Don’t want to draft the hero? 
The Banker must auction the hero’s allegiance. Bidding starts 
at 10, and anyone can increase the bid by as little as 1. You 
don’t need to follow turn order. The Banker ends the auction 
when no player is willing to increase the bid. The highest

How do I get out of Jail?
You have 3 options:
1. Pay 50 at the start of your next turn; then, roll 
and move as normal. 
2. Use a Get Out of Jail Free card at the start of 
your next turn if you have one (or buy one from 
another player). Put the card at the bottom of the 
appropriate deck; then, roll and move. 
3. Roll doubles on your next turn. If you do, you’re 
free! Use the roll to move, and that’s the end of 
your turn.   
You can use up to 3 turns to try for doubles. If 
you don’t roll doubles by your third turn In Jail, 
pay 50, and use your last roll to move.
