BUILDINGS HELP! I CAN’T PAY!
Building Villages
As soon as you get a colour set, you can start 
building Villages (you don’t have to wait for  
your turn). 
Pay the Reserve the cost on the Title Deed card 
and place a Village on the location.
You must build evenly. You cannot build a second 
Village on a location until you’ve built one on 
each location in the set. You can only have  
4 Villages on a location. 
Building Palaces
Once you have four Villages on all locations in a 
colour set, you can pay to upgrade to a Palace.
Pay the Palace cost on the Title Deed card, 
return all four Villages to the Reserve and put  
a Palace on the location.
You can only have 1 Palace per location.  
You can’t add any more Villages.
You cannot build on a location if any location  
in its colour set is mortgaged.
1  Try to raise cosmic energy units.
  If you owe cosmic energy units and can’t pay, try to 
raise cosmic energy units by selling buildings back  
to the Reserve and/or mortgaging properties.
 Selling buildings 
  Sell Palaces to the Reserve for half the cost,  
and exchange them immediately for 4 Villages.
  Sell Villages to the Reserve for half the cost.  
Villages must be sold evenly across the colour set.
 Mortgaging property 
  To mortgage a property, you must first sell all buildings 
in its colour set to the Reserve at half their cost price. 
  To mortgage, turn the Title Deed card face down,  
and collect the mortgage value on the back from  
the Reserve. 
  To repay a mortgage, pay the unmortgage cost  
to the Reserve (mortgage value +10%), then turn  
the card face up.
2   If you’re still in debt, you are out of the 
game!
  Do you owe another player? 
 Give them all your mortgaged properties and any Get 
Out of Jail Free cards.
  The new owner must immediately: 
 Repay the mortgage (pay the Reserve the 
unmortgage cost).  
Or keep the mortgage (just pay the Reserve 10% 
of the mortgage value now).
 Do you owe the Reserve?
  Return all your properties to the Reserve. Any 
mortgages are cancelled.
  All your properties must immediately be put up for 
auction. 
  Return any Get Out of Jail Free cards to the bottom of 
the appropriate deck.
  The remaining players keep playing until 
there is only one person 
left in the game.
  That player is the 
winner!
DEALS & TRADES
You can buy, sell or swap property with 
other players at any time. 
You must sell all buildings on a colour set to 
the Reserve before you can sell or trade a 
location. You cannot sell or trade buildings 
to another player.
Property can be traded for cosmic energy 
units, other property, and/or Get Out of Jail 
Free cards. The amount is decided by the 
players making the deal.
Mortgaged property can be traded at any 
agreed price.
The new owner must immediately:
Repay the mortgage (pay the Reserve  
the unmortgage cost).
Or keep the mortgage (just pay the 
Reserve 10% of the mortgage value now).
Rent cannot be collected on properties that are 
mortgaged. However, the increased rent level can be 
collected on the unmortgaged locations in a colour set.
The increased rent on unmortgaged transports and 
celestial devices may be collected in the same way.
TOP TIPS 
To keep your game short and sweet, don’t use house rules!
Always auction when someone doesn’t want to safeguard the property they’ve landed on.
Never loan cosmic energy units to other players or make deals not to charge each other rent. 
Never put cosmic energy units in the centre of the board; you don’t get a bonus for landing on Free Parking!
Aim of the Game
Go on a journey through time as one of ten Eternals: beings with Super Hero abilities 
who have secretly protected the human race for centuries. Safeguard locations  
and collect rent from fellow Eternals in the form of cosmic energy units. The player 
with cosmic energy units when all other players have depleted theirs wins!

THE BOARD SPACES
PROPERTIES
There are three types of properties: locations, which come in colour sets, transports and celestial devices.
Unsafeguarded Properties
When you land on an unsafeguarded location, transport or celestial device, you must safeguard it or auction it.
 Want to safeguard it?
 Pay the price on the board space and take the Title Deed card from the Reserve.
 Don’t want to safeguard it?
  The Leader must auction it. Bidding starts at 10, and anyone can increase the bid 
by as little as 1. You don’t need to follow turn order, and the Leader ends the 
auction when no player is willing to increase the bid. The highest bidder 
pays the Reserve. If no one wants to bid on the property, that’s fine. 
No one pays anything and the Title Deed stays with the Reserve. 
Collect colour sets!
When you safeguard each location in a colour set: 
• You can double the rent for those locations! 
•  You may build Villages and Palaces and charge  
even more rent! See BUILDINGS.
Safeguarded Properties
When you land on a property that someone else 
safeguarded, that player must ask you for rent.  
If they do, you must pay. If they don’t ask before  
the next player rolls the dice, you don’t have to pay!

GO 
When you pass or land on 
the GO space, collect 200 
from the Reserve.
How do I get out of Jail?
You have three options:
1.  Pay 50 at the start of your next turn, then roll and 
move as normal.
2.  Use a Get Out of Jail Free card at the start of 
your next turn if you have one (or buy one from another 
player). Put the card at the bottom of the appropriate 
deck, then roll and move. 
3.  Roll a double on your next turn. If you do, you’re free! 
Use the roll to move, and that’s the end of your turn. 
  You can use up to three turns to try for a double. If you 
don’t roll a double by your third turn in Jail, pay 50, and 
use your last roll to move.

ACTION SPACES
SUPER HERO ABILITIES 
Each character has a unique Super Hero ability!  
When you roll to move, if you roll , activate your ability!
For most characters, after you activate your ability, you’ll use the two white 
dice to move and take your turn as normal. For some characters, the ability 
will change the way you move. When that is the case, move according to 
the rules of your ability. 
Ikaris – Draw three Uni-Mind cards. Choose one and immediately do what 
it says. Place all three cards at the bottom of the deck when you've finished. 
Sersi – You may change one of your white dice to any number from 1 to 6. 
Ajak – Draw three Arishem’s Judgement cards. Choose one and 
immediately do what it says. Place all three cards at the bottom of the deck 
when you've finished.
Phastos – Send any other player to GO. That player collects 200 and 
gives you 100.
Makkari – Add 7 to your movement. 
Druig – Move to the next Arishem’s Judgement space. Take the top card 
and immediately do what it says. Place the card at the bottom of the deck 
when you've finished. Do not move according to the two white dice. 
Gilgamesh – Move to the next Uni-Mind space. Take the top card and 
immediately do what it says. Place the card at the bottom of the deck when 
you've finished. Do not move according to the two white dice.
Thena – Follow the rules of the space where you land. Then roll all three 
dice and move again (as long as you are not in Jail)! You may roll  up to 
four times in a row. On your fourth time rolling , go to Jail. 
Kingo – You may change one of your white dice to match the other.  
For example, if you roll a  and a , you may choose to change the   
to a  and move eight spaces.  
Sprite – Move any other player to Free Parking. If they pass GO, they do 
not collect 200.
PLAY!
How to win
Move around the board safeguarding as 
many properties as you can. The more you 
safeguard, the more rent you’ll be able 
to collect in the form of cosmic energy 
units. If you’re the last player with cosmic 
energy units when all other players have 
depleted theirs, you win!
Who goes first?
Each player rolls the two white dice. 
The highest roller starts and play moves 
to the left.
On your turn
1.  Roll all three dice.
2.  If you roll  on the golden cosmic die, 
activate your character’s unique ability! 
See SUPER HERO ABILITIES to the right 
or your character card for details.
3.  Move your piece clockwise the number 
of spaces shown on the two white dice 
unless you activated your ability and 
that ability tells you to move differently. 
4.  Where did you land? Carry out the rules 
of that board space. See THE BOARD 
SPACES. 
Note, in this version of MONOPOLY,  
you do not take another turn when  
you roll doubles! 
5.  Your turn ends. Pass the dice to  
your left.
