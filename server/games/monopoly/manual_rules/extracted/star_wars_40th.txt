HELP! I CAN’T PAY!
Lending money between players, receiving cash for landing on Free Parking, and never auctioning 
properties are just some of the “house” rules that lengthen the game.
To keep your game short and sweet, don’t use house rules!
Always auction when someone doesn’t want to buy the property they’ve landed on.
Never loan money to other players, or make deals to not charge each other rent.
Never put cash in the center of the board; you don’t get a bonus for landing on Free Parking!

GAME GUIDE
Building Fleets
As soon as you get a color set, you 
can start buying Fleets (you don’t have 
to wait for your turn) and showing your allegiance to 
either the Galactic Empire or Rebel Alliance.
•  Pay the Bank the Fleet cost price on the Title Deed 
card and place a Fleet on the Scene.
•  Fleets should be stacked one on top of the other, 
with the Empire or Rebel symbol faceup to show 
other players where your loyalties lie!
•  Build evenly. You cannot build a second Fleet on a 
Scene until you’ve built one on each Scene in its  
color set. You can only have 4 Fleets on a Scene.
Building Bases
Once you have 4 Fleets on all Scenes  
in a color set, you can pay to upgrade  
to a Base.
•  Pay the Bank the Base cost price on the Title Deed 
card, return all 4 Fleets to the Bank, and put a Base 
on the Scene.
•  You can only build 1 Base per Scene. You can’t add 
any more Fleets.
•  Ensure that the Galactic Empire or Rebel Alliance 
symbol on your Base is facing outward so other 
players can see whose side you’re on!
You cannot build on a Scene if any Scene in its color 
set is mortgaged.
Not enough buildings?
If multiple players want the last Fleet or Base, the 
Banker must auction it. Bids start at A10.
No buildings left?
You can’t buy any until someone sells theirs back.
Auctions
If you land on an unowned Scene, Ship, or Power 
Source and you don’t want to buy it, the Banker must 
auction it.
1.  The Banker starts the auction by offering the space 
to everyone for A10.
2.  Anyone can increase the bid by as little as A1  
(even the Banker and the player who originally 
landed on the space).
3.  The highest bidder wins the auction, pays the Bank, 
and takes the Title Deed card.
What if no one wants it?
That’s fine. No one pays anything.
Deals & Trades
You can buy, sell, or swap property with other players  
at any time.
You must sell all Fleets and Bases on a color set to the  
Bank before you can sell or trade a Scene. You cannot 
sell (or trade) Fleets or Bases to another player.
Property can be traded for cash, other property, and/ 
or Get Out of Jail Free cards. The amount is decided by 
the players making the deal. Who knows, you might cut 
a better deal if a player shares your allegiance to either 
the Galactic Empire or Rebel Alliance!
Mortgaged property can be traded at any agreed price.
The new owner must immediately either:
Repay the mortgage (pay the Bank the unmortgage 
cost). Or keep the mortgage (just pay the Bank 10% 
of the mortgage value now).
BUILDINGS
If you’re still in debt, you are bankrupt and out of  
the game!
Do you owe another player?
Give them any 
Get Out of Jail Free cards and all your 
mortgaged properties.
The new owner must immediately either:
Repay the mortgage (pay the Bank the  
unmortgage cost).
Or keep the mortgage (just pay the Bank 10% of  
the mortgage value now).
Do you owe the Bank?
Return all your properties to the Bank. Any mortgages 
are cancelled.
All your properties must immediately be put up  
for auction. 
Return any Get Out of Jail Free cards to the bottom  
of the pile.
The remaining players keep playing until there is  
only one person left in the game—the winner! 
Try to raise money.
If you owe money and can’t pay, try to raise money  
by selling buildings and/or mortgaging properties. 
Selling Buildings
Sell Bases to the Bank for half the cost price and 
exchange them immediately for 4 Fleets.
Sell Fleets to the Bank for half the cost price.  
Fleets must be sold evenly across the color set. 
Mortgaging Property
To mortgage a property, you must first sell all 
buildings in its color set to the Bank at half their  
cost price. 
To mortgage, turn the Title Deed card facedown and 
collect the mortgage value on the back from the Bank. 
To repay a mortgage, pay the unmortgage cost to  
the Bank (mortgage value +10%), then turn the  
card faceup.
Rent cannot be collected on properties that are 
mortgaged. However, the increased rent level can be 
collected on the unmortgaged Scenes in a color set.
The increased rent on unmortgaged Ships and 
Powers Sources may be collected in the same way.

Looks like you’ve found a quiet corner of the galaxy! Nothing

Don’t worry, you’ve avoided the wrath of the Empire and are not 
In Jail. Simply put your token in the Just Visiting section.
Go To Jail
You’ve displeased the Empire! Move your token to the In Jail 
space immediately! Do not collect A200 for passing GO. Your 
turn is then over. You can still collect rent, auction, buy Fleets 
and Bases, mortgage, and trade while you are In Jail.
Scenes
Unowned Scenes
When you land on a Scene that no one owns, either buy it 
or auction it.
How do I get out of Jail?
You have 3 options:
1  Pay b50 at the start of your next turn, then roll and move as normal.
2  Use a Get Out of Jail Free card at the start of your next turn if you have 
one (or buy one from another player). Put the card at the bottom of the 
appropriate pile, then roll and move.
3  Roll a double on your next turn. If you do, you’re free to roam the galaxy 
again! Use the roll to move, and that’s the end of your turn. You can use 
up to 3 turns to try for a double. If you don’t throw a double by your third 
turn In Jail, pay A50 and use your last roll to move.
