Building Lairs
Once you have 4 Thickets on all contracts in 
a color set, you can pay to upgrade to a Lair.
Pay the Lair cost on the contract card, return 
all 4 Thickets to the Bank, and put a Lair on 
the contract.
You can only have 1 Lair per contract. You can’t 
add any more Thickets.
Not enough buildings?
If multiple players want to buy the last Thicket or Lair, the Banker 
must auction it. Bids start at a1 and anyone can increase the bid 
by as little as a1. You don’t need to follow turn order.
Payment goes to the Bank.
No buildings left? 
You can’t buy any until someone sells theirs back.

Go to Jail 
Move your token to the In Jail 
space immediately! Do not 
collect a2 for passing GO. Your 
turn is then over. You may still 
collect rent, bid during auctions, 
buy Thickets and Lairs, and 
trade while you are in Jail.
How do I get out of Jail?
You have 3 options:
1. Pay b5 at the start of your next turn, then 
roll and move as normal.
2. Use a Get Out of Jail Free card at the start 
of your next turn if you have one (or buy one 
from another player). Put the card at the 
bottom of the appropriate deck, then roll 
and move.  
3. Roll a 6 on your next turn. If you do, you’re 
free! Use the roll to move, and that’s the end 
of your turn.  
You can use up to three turns to try for a 6. 
If you don’t roll a 6 by your third turn in Jail, 
pay a5, and use your last roll to move.

PROPERTIES
Unowned Properties
When you land on a contract no one owns, you must buy it! 
Pay the price on the board space, and take that contract card. If 
you can’t afford to pay, you are bankrupt and out of the game! 
Return any cards to the bottom of the appropriate deck.
When you land on a utility no one owns, you must buy it! 
Pay the price on the board space, and take that utility 
card. If you can’t afford to pay, you are bankrupt and 
out of the game! Return any cards to the bottom of 
the appropriate deck.
There are two types of properties: villain contracts, which come in color 
sets, and utilities, which are Spells and Potions.  
Owned Properties
When you land on a property that someone else owns, the owner 
must ask you for rent. If they do, you must pay. If they don’t ask 
before the next player rolls the die, you don’t have to pay!
