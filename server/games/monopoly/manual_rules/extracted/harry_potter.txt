YOUR JOURNEY TO HOGWARTS
™
 HAS BEGUN! 
W elcome to Hogwarts™ castle and the nearby Hogsmeade™ village! Choose a house and earn house points as 
you travel around the board to your favourite magical locations. Explore classrooms and common rooms, add 
house crests, and receive post from the school owl. But watch out for Howlers—they’ll leave you steaming! 
The game ends when all properties have been explored. The player with the most house points wins!
HOUSE CRESTS
Adding house crests
As soon as you get a colour set, you 
can start adding house crests (you 
don’t have to wait for your turn). 
Give the Bank the cost on the Title 
Deed card, and place a house crest on 
the location.
Y ou may only have 1 house crest on 
a location.
Not enough house crests?
If multiple players want to add the last house crest, the Prefect must 
auction it. Bids start at 10 house points, and anyone can increase 
the bid by as little as 10 house points. Y ou don’t need to follow turn 
order. House points go to the Bank. 
No house crests left? 
Y ou can’t add any until someone returns theirs to the Bank. 
T ry to raise house points.
Return house crests to the Bank and/or mortgage properties 
in exchange for house points.
Returning house crests
Return house crests to the Bank for their cost.
Mortgaging Property
T o mortgage a property, you must first return all house 
crests in its colour set to the Bank for their cost. Then, turn 
the Title Deed card facedown, and receive the mortgage 
value on the back from the Bank.
T o end a mortgage, give the Bank the unmortgage cost 
listed on the Title Deed card, then turn the card faceup.
Y ou cannot receive rent on properties that are 
mortgaged. However, you can receive the increased 
rent level on the unmortgaged locations in a colour set.
Y ou may receive the increased rent on unmortgaged 
common rooms and spells in the same way. 
If you still owe house points, you’re 
out of luck and out of the game!
Do you owe another player?
Give them all your mortgaged properties and any Get 
Out of Jail Free cards.
The new owner may immediately end the mortgage 
or do so on a later turn.
Do you owe the Bank?
Return all your properties to the Bank. Any 
mortgages are cancelled.
All your properties must immediately be put up 
for auction. 
Return any Get Out of Jail Free cards to the bottom of 
the deck.
THE END OF THE GAME
The game ends immediately when all the properties have 
been explored.
Then players do the following:
• All players receive house points from the Bank for 
each of their explored properties (i.e., the amount 
shown on each board space).
o  For locations in a colour set, receive double the 
house points!
o  If any of those locations have a house crest, 
receive the rent with house crest value shown on 
their Title Deed cards instead.
Now count up your house points. The player with the 
most wins!

Y ou’d end up with a total of

I OWE HOUSE POINTS, BUT DON’T HA VE ENOUGH! HELPFUL HINTS FROM THE PREFECTS
T o keep your game short and sweet, don’t provoke Filch by breaking the rules!
Always auction when someone doesn’t want to explore the property they’ve landed on.
Never loan house points to other players or make deals not to receive rent from each other. 
Never put house points in the centre of the board; you don’t get rewarded for landing on Free Parking!
DEALS & TRADES
At any time, you can:
• Reward other players with house points in 
exchange for their properties.
• Receive house points from other players in 
exchange for your properties.
• T rade properties with other players.
Y ou must return all house crests on a colour set to the 
Bank in exchange for their cost before you can receive 
house points for a location in a deal or trade a location.
Property can be traded for house points, other 
property, and/or Get Out of Jail Free cards. The 
amount is decided by the players making the deal.
Mortgaged property can be traded at any agreed-
upon amount.
The new owner may immediately give the Bank the 
unmortgage cost listed on the Title Deed card or 
wait to unmortgage the property on a later turn.

PROPERTIES
There are three types of properties: locations, which come in colour sets, common rooms, and spells. 
Unexplored Properties
When you land on an unexplored property, you must explore it or auction it. 
W ant to explore it? 
Give the amount of house points shown on the board space to the Bank, and then 
take the Title Deed card. 
Don’t want to explore it? Auction it!
The Prefect must auction it. Bidding starts at 10 house points, and anyone can 
increase the bid by as little as 10 house points . Y ou don’t need to follow turn order, and 
the Prefect ends the auction when no player is willing to increase the bid. The highest bidder 
gives the house points they bid to the Bank. If no one wants to bid on the property, that’s fine. 
No one does anything, and the Title Deed stays with the Bank.
Collect colour sets!
When you have explored each location in a colour set: 
• Y ou can double the rent for those locations! 
• Y ou may add house crests and charge even more rent! 
See HOUSE CRESTS.
Explored Properties
When you land on a property that someone else has explored, the owner must ask you 
for rent. If they do, you reward them with house points. If they don’t ask before the next 
player rolls the dice, you don’t give them anything!

How do I get out of Jail?
Y ou have 3 options:
1.  Give 10 house points to the Bank at the start of your 
next turn, then roll and move as normal.
2.  Use a Get Out of Jail Free card at the start of your next 
turn if you have one (or get one from another player by 
giving them house points in return). Put the card at the 
bottom of the deck, then roll and move. 
3.  Roll doubles on your next turn. If you do, you’re free! 
Use the roll to move, and that’s the end of your turn. 
Y ou can use up to 3 turns to try for doubles. If you don’t 
roll doubles by your third turn in Jail, give 10 house 
points to the Bank, and use your last roll to move.
