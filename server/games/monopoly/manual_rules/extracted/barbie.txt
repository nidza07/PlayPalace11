GO
When you pass or land on the 
GO space, collect a200 from 
the Bank.
Free Parking
Relax! Nothing happens. 
Practice your interviewing skills 
or browse your fave online 
upcycled clothing boutique.
Just Visiting
Don’t worry. If you land here, 
put your token in the Just 
Visiting section. 
Go to Jail 
Immediately move your token to the In Jail 
space! Do not collect a200 for passing 
GO. Your turn is then over. You can still 
collect rent, bid during auctions, buy 
DreamHouses, and trade while you are in 
Jail. You cannot use your Be Anything skill.
How do I get out of Jail?
You have 3 options:
1. Pay a50 at the start of your next turn, then roll and  
 move as normal.
2. Use a Get Out of Jail Free card at the start of your  
 next turn if you have one (or buy one from another  
 player). Put the card at the bottom of the appropriate  
 deck, then roll and move. 
3. Roll doubles on your next turn. If you do, you’re free!  
 Use the roll to move, and that’s the end of your turn. 
You can use up to 3 turns to try for doubles. If you don’t 
roll doubles by your third turn in Jail, pay a50, and use 
your last roll to move.

CAREER
Dream Career and Dream Closet cards allow you to experience some of the most iconic Barbie jobs 
and fashion moments. Some of these cards can be kept until you are ready to use them, while other 
cards must be used immediately. You may only keep up to two Dream Career or Dream Closet cards 
at any time. Place any cards you don’t want to keep at the bottom of the appropriate deck.
If you already have two Dream Career or Dream Closet cards and you draw another one, 
you may exchange one of the cards you already have for the new card, or simply return 
the new card to the bottom of the appropriate deck. However, if you draw a Dream 
Career or Dream Closet card that must be used immediately, you must do so. If you 
already have two cards, you can keep them both—it won’t count as having three cards!
Transports
If you land on a Transport 
space, you may move to any 
street between that Transport 
and the next one. If you pass 
GO, collect a200.
As soon as you get a color set, you can buy DreamHouses (you don’t have to wait for your 
turn). Pay the Bank the cost on the Title Deed card, and place a DreamHouse on the street. 
You can only have 1 DreamHouse per street. 
You can buy, sell, or trade streets with other players at any time. 
You must sell both DreamHouses on a color set to the Bank before you can sell or trade a street. 
You cannot sell or trade DreamHouses to another player.
Streets can be traded for cash, other streets, and/or Get Out of Jail Free cards. The amount is 
decided by the players making the deal.
1. Try to raise money.
 If you owe money and can’t pay, try to raise money by selling DreamHouses or  
 streets back to the Bank. You may also sell streets to other players.
 Selling DreamHouses
 Sell DreamHouses to the Bank for half the cost.
 Selling Streets
 Sell streets to another player at an agreed-upon price, or back to the Bank for half  
 the cost.
2. If you’re still in debt, you are bankrupt and the game ends!
The game ends immediately when the first player goes bankrupt. 
Then the remaining players each do the following:
 1. Collect rent from the Bank for each of your streets.
  Remember to double your rent for each color set you have, and to   
  collect rent for DreamHouses!
 2. Count up your cash.
The player with the most money wins!
To keep your game short and sweet, don’t use 
house rules!
Always auction when someone doesn’t want to 
buy the street they’ve landed on.
Never loan money to other players or make deals 
not to charge each other rent. 
Never put cash in the center of the board; you 
don’t get a bonus for landing on Free Parking!
HELP! I CAN’T PAY!
WIN!

Want to buy it? 
Pay the price on the board space, and take the 
Title Deed card from the Bank. 
Don’t want to buy it? Auction it!
The Banker must auction it. Bidding starts at 
a10, and anyone can increase the bid by as little 
as a1. You don’t need to follow turn order, and 
the Banker ends the auction when no player is 
willing to increase the bid. The highest bidder 
pays the Bank. If no one wants to bid on the 
street, that’s fine. No one pays anything, and the 
Title Deed stays with the Bank. 
Collect color sets! 
When you own each street in a color set: 
 • You can double the rent for those streets! 
 • You may build DreamHouses and charge even more rent! 
  See BUILDING DREAMHOUSES.
Owned Streets
When you land on a street that someone else owns, the owner 
must ask you for rent. If they do, you must pay. If they don’t ask 
before the next player rolls the dice, you don’t have to pay!
