TOP TIPS
To keep your game short and sweet, 
don’t use house rules!
Always auction when someone doesn’t want to 
buy the property they’ve landed on.
Never loan money to other players or make 
deals not to charge each other rent.
Never put cash in the centre of the board; you 
don’t get a bonus for landing on Free Parking!
If you’re still in debt, you are 
bankrupt and out of the game!
Return all your properties to the Bank.

back of the Destiny deck.
Do you owe another player?
Do you owe the Bank?
The remaining players keep playing 
until there is only one person left in 
the game. That player is the winner!
Placing Beetles
Once you have 4 Grubs on all scenes in a  
colour set, you can pay to upgrade to a Beetle.
Pay the Beetle cost shown on the Title Deed 
card, return all 4 Grubs to the Bank and put a 
Beetle on the scene. You can only have 1 Beetle 
per scene. You can’t add any more Grubs.
You cannot place Grubs or Beetles on a scene if 
any scene in its colour set is mortgaged. 
Not enough bugs?
If several players want to buy the last Grub or Beetle,  
the Banker must auction it. Bids start at a 10, and anyone 
can increase the bid by as little as a1. You don’t need to 
follow turn order. Payment goes to the Bank. 
No bugs left? 
You can’t buy any until someone sells theirs back.
As soon as you get a colour set, you can 
start buying Grubs (you don’t have to wait 
for your turn).   
Pay the Bank the amount shown on the Title 
Deed card and place a Grub on the scene.
You must place Grubs evenly. You cannot 
place a second Grub on a scene until you’ve 
placed one on each scene in the set. You 
can only have 4 Grubs on a scene.

How do I get out of Jail?
You have 3 options:
1. Pay b50 at the beginning of your next 
turn, then roll and move as normal. 
2. Use a Get Out of Jail Free card at the 
beginning of your next turn, if you have 
one (or buy one from another player). 
Put the card at the back of the Destiny 
deck, then roll and move.
3. Roll doubles on your next turn.  
If you do, you’re free! Use the roll to 
move, and that’s the end of your turn.  
You can use up to 3 turns to try for 
doubles. If you don’t roll doubles by your 
third turn in Jail, pay a 50, and use your 
last roll to move.

Unowned Properties
When you land on an unowned property, you must buy it or put it up for auction.
Want to buy it? 
Pay the price shown on the board space and take the Title Deed card from the Bank.  
Don’t want to buy it? 
The Banker must auction it. Bids start at a 10, and anyone can increase a bid by as little as 
a1. You don’t need to follow turn order, and the Banker ends the auction when nobody  
is willing to increase the bid. The highest bidder pays the Bank. If nobody wants to bid on 
the property, that’s fine. Nobody pays anything, and the Title Deed stays with the Bank.
